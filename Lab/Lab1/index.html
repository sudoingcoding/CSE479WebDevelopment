A dramatization of the life story of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the physicist who had a large hand in the development of the atomic bombs that brought an end to World War II. The movie highlights the ups and downs of Oppenheimer and the inner workings of the secret Manhattan Project during that time.

Storyline

In 1926, young physicist J. Robert Oppenheimer begins his journey in quantum physics, ultimately joining the Manhattan Project to lead the development of the atomic bomb during World War II. Despite success, he is haunted by the bomb's devastating effects. Post-war, his opposition to nuclear escalation and ties to communist acquaintances bring him under scrutiny, resulting in a revoked security clearance in a biased hearing. Although later recognized for his contributions, Oppenheimer’s legacy is clouded by the ethical weight of his work and the fears of its long-term consequences.

Cast

The film features an ensemble cast, with Cillian Murphy taking on the intense and complex role of J. Robert Oppenheimer. Known for his remarkable ability to portray deep, introspective characters, Murphy brings Oppenheimer’s internal struggles and intellectual brilliance to life.

Supporting Murphy are talented actors such as Emily Blunt, Matt Damon, and Robert Downey Jr., who add depth and nuance to the story, each representing key figures in Oppenheimer's life and the Manhattan Project.

  • Cillian Murphy as J. Robert Oppenheimer
  • Emily Blunt as Katherine "Kitty" Oppenheimer
  • Matt Damon as Colonel Leslie Groves
  • Robert Downey Jr. as Lewis Strauss
  • Florence Pugh as Jean Tatlock
  • Rami Malek as David L. Hill
  • Benny Safdie as Edward Teller
